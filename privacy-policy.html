Introduction

Welcome to Wryt - AI Grammar & Spell Checker. We are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring transparency about how our browser extension works. This Privacy Policy explains what data we collect, how we use it, and your rights regarding your information.

By using the Wryt extension, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy.

Required Permissions

Our extension requires the following permissions to function properly:

storage

Why we need it: To save your preferences, API keys, and extension settings locally on your device. This allows the extension to remember your configuration and preferred AI providers across browser sessions.

activeTab

Why we need it: To access and analyze the text you select on the active webpage. This permission allows the extension to read selected text when you right-click and choose "Check Grammar & Spelling."

scripting

Why we need it: To inject our grammar checking user interface into webpages, allowing you to see corrections and suggestions directly on the page where you're writing.

contextMenus

Why we need it: To add our functionality to the right-click context menu, providing quick access to grammar checking and AI text generation features.

Host Permissions

Why we need it: To communicate with AI provider APIs for grammar checking and text generation. We request access to the following domains:

  • generativelanguage.googleapis.com - Google Gemini API
  • api.groq.com - Groq API
  • openrouter.ai - OpenRouter API
  • models.inference.ai.azure.com - Azure AI Inference
  • api.cerebras.ai - Cerebras API
  • api.sambanova.ai - SambaNova API

Information We Collect

Data We Collect:

  • User Preferences: Your chosen tone (formal, casual, neutral), dialect (US, UK, CA), brand voice guidelines, and plagiarism check settings.
  • API Keys: API keys you provide for AI providers. These are stored locally on your device using Chrome's storage API.
  • Selected Text: Text you select and submit for grammar checking. This text is temporarily sent to your configured AI provider for analysis.
  • Extension Usage: We track which AI providers are enabled and their priority order to implement our waterfall failover logic.

Data We DO NOT Collect:

  • ❌ Your browsing history
  • ❌ Personal identifying information (name, email, address, etc.)
  • ❌ Passwords or sensitive credentials
  • ❌ Data from pages you don't actively use the extension on
  • ❌ Analytics or tracking data

How We Use Your Information

The information we collect is used solely to:

  • Provide grammar and spell checking services by sending your selected text to AI providers
  • Remember your preferences and settings across browser sessions
  • Implement intelligent failover between AI providers to ensure service reliability
  • Generate text based on your prompts when using the AI writing feature
Important: We do NOT sell, share, or distribute your data to third parties for marketing or advertising purposes. Your data is used exclusively for the functionality of this extension.

Data Storage & Security

Local Storage: All user preferences and API keys are stored locally on your device using Chrome's chrome.storage.sync API. This data is synchronized across your Chrome browsers if you're signed in to Chrome, but it never passes through our servers because we don't operate any servers.

API Communication: When you check grammar or generate text, your selected text is sent directly from your browser to the AI provider you've configured (Google Gemini, Groq, OpenRouter, etc.) over HTTPS encrypted connections. We do not intercept, store, or log this communication.

No Backend: Wryt operates entirely client-side. We do not have servers that collect, store, or process your data.

User Rights

You have full control over your data:

  • Access: View your stored preferences at any time by opening the extension settings.
  • Modification: Change your API keys, preferences, and settings whenever you want through the extension options page.
  • Deletion: Remove all stored data by uninstalling the extension or clearing your browser's extension data. You can also manually delete individual API keys and preferences in the settings.

We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy periodically. Continued use of the extension after changes constitute your acceptance of the updated policy.
